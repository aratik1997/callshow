How to Play

Goal

Score as few points as possible over the course of the game by ending rounds with a low hand. Cross 200 points and you're eliminated — the last player left wins.

Card values

Ace = 1  ·  2–10 = face value  ·  J/Q/K = 10  ·  Joker = 0

Your turn

Everyone starts each round with 5 cards. On your turn you throw first, then draw:

  1. Throw one of the following combinations from your hand:
    • Single — any one card
    • Set — 2 or more cards of the same rank
    • Run — 3 or more consecutive cards of the same suit (e.g. 5♠-6♠-7♠)
    Jokers are wild and can complete a set or run.
  2. Then draw one card to refill your hand — either from the deck, or from either end of the Table pile. The Table pile is always whatever the previous player threw — never your own throw. As soon as you throw, everyone can see your cards appear under "Just Played"; once you draw, they move to the Table pile for the next player.

Calling

Whenever your hand value drops below 11 (checked right after you draw), you're automatically marked Called — everyone sees a badge on your pod and a pop-up announcing it. Calling doesn't end the round; play just continues.

Showing

On a later turn — after you've thrown and drawn again — if your hand is still under 11, you get the option to Show:

  • Show reveals every player's hand. If yours is the lowest, you score 0 and everyone else scores their own hand total.
  • If anyone else ties or beats your total, that's an Asaf! — you score your total + 30 penalty points instead, while everyone (including whoever caught you) simply scores their own hand total as usual.
  • Don't Show — stay called and keep playing. You'll get another chance to show on a future turn as long as your hand stays under 11.

If your hand ever climbs back to 11 or more, your call is cancelled — and without an active call, you can't show at all. You'll need to drop under 11 again to re-call.

Scores

Running scores are hidden during play — you'll only see hand totals and round points after each round. Full scores are revealed once the game finishes.

Winning the game

Play continues round after round. Landing on exactly 100 drops you to 50; landing on exactly 200 drops you to 100. Go over 200 and you're eliminated. Last player standing wins the game!

Other notes

  • Up to 8 players per room, no accounts needed — just a name and a 5-letter room code.
  • If a player leaves mid-game, everyone is notified and they're removed from the table. If only one player remains, they win by default.
